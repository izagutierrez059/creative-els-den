“Bloom Anyway”

Medium: Watercolor Painting
Created: April 2021

There’s something quietly rebellious about blooming in a space that isn’t made for growth. “Bloom Anyway” was created during a time when I felt emotionally stuck—when everything around me seemed dim, cluttered, and uninspired. But somewhere deep inside, I still longed for softness. I still longed for color.

This piece became a gentle defiance. The flower at its center pushes through unseen weight—symbolizing resilience not born from ease, but from choosing to rise anyway. The muted earth tones and soft pastels reflect a quiet strength, while the layered texture speaks of scars, healing, and beginnings that aren't necessarily loud or linear.

The imperfect edges, the rawness, the unpolished finish—these are intentional. Because growth is never neat. Sometimes it’s messy, slow, and uncertain. But even then, it’s still growth.
